Team Rule When we lose the ball, everyone drops back into shape before pressing. Chasing in ones and twos causes chaos.

Central play basics

The DM screens in front of the back line — never abandons the centre channel. CMs work in rotation: one forward, one holds. Strikers stay narrow to pin CBs and create space for late runs.

In possession

GK distributes short. CBs push wide to create triangles. DM drops between CBs. One CM drops to receive, the other drives forward. Strikers make diagonal runs when CM carries ball.

Out of possession

Everyone drops into shape first. Strikers press their CBs high. CMs press wide, force inside. DM holds the centre. Back three hold their channels — never all three push at once.

Why no wide play?

On a 9-a-side pitch, crossing angles are tighter and fewer players attack the box. Central play gives shorter pass distances, more triangles, and keeps the box from getting crowded.
